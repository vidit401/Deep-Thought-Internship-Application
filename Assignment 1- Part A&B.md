- 3-Key decisions (sent in internshala chat window as well):

 -1. I decided to put the story part so that the paragraph about what happened comes before the charts and numbers. This way the portfolio feels like a story that we can show to a mentor, instead of a bunch of data on a dashboard to track progress. The supervisors might like to see the numbers because it is faster, but this way is more efficient for understanding the feel of the performance.

 -2. I made a chart that shows how an employee is doing in different areas. It is clear that the employee is doing well in Delivery, where they got the score of 80, but the employee needs to work on Frameworks, where they only got a score of 52. The chart also has a line at 60, which shows the supervisor and the employee where they need to improve. This makes it easy to see what the employee needs to work on without having to read through a lot of numbers.

 -3. I made the Constraints such that they are posed as ‘What blocked progress’ with resolution rate first (62% resolved). This turns blockers into diagnostic data, not failures, so employees feel safe being honest about their obstacles.
